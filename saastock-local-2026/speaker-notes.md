# Speaker Notes — "The Agent IS the Product"
## SaaStock Local London | March 25, 2026

### Slide 1: Title
- Wait for audience to settle. Smile. No rush.

### Slide 2: About Me
- "I'm Romain, CEO and co-founder of StackOne."
- Point to the journey: "I'm French. Moved to London to build StackOne. Moved to SF. Came back to London. And now I'm back in SF again. I keep following where the most interesting things in software are happening."
- "Right now, the most interesting thing happening is that entire categories are being rebuilt from scratch. That's what I want to talk about tonight."
- Keep it light — 60 seconds max. The journey is relatable to an international founder audience.

### Slide 3: A New Breed of Companies
- "Over the past 18 months, a new breed of company has emerged."
- Go through each row in the table. Slow. Let each one land:
  - Sierra: "Didn't build a better Zendesk..."
  - Cursor: "$2 billion ARR in 26 months. The fastest-scaling software company in history."
  - Harvey: "50 of the top 100 US law firms."
  - Lovable: "$100M ARR in 8 months. Fastest in European history."
  - Rillet: "Rebuilt the general ledger from scratch."
  - Garfield.Law: Slow down here. "First UK regulator-approved AI-only law firm. Here. In London." Pause.
- "None of these companies existed three years ago. But to build like this requires some fundamental mental shifts. Let me walk through four."

### Slide 4: Accuracy > Determinism
- "The first shift is the hardest for enterprise founders to accept."
- Explain: 30 years of deterministic = enterprise-grade. AI breaks that assumption.
- "But if an AI resolves 95% of tickets correctly and humans resolve 85% — the probabilistic system wins. On outcomes."
- Sierra example: agents reason, not follow scripts. Abridge: 150+ health systems trust it.
- Land on: "Probabilistic reasoning with deterministic guardrails. Agents explore and decide. Rules enforce and validate."

### Slide 5: Usage > Subscription
- "The second shift is about how software gets paid for."
- Walk through the pricing evolution. "Seat-based pricing dropped from 21% to 15% in just 12 months."
- "When the agent does the work, charging per seat makes no sense. Who's the seat for?"
- Point to examples: Sierra, Intercom, Workday, HubSpot, Adobe — all moving.

### Slide 6: Agentic Cost < Traditional TCO
- "The third shift is the economic one — and it's why this is all happening so fast."
- "When you buy Zendesk, you pay for software AND the team. When you buy Sierra, you pay per resolution. The agent IS the team."
- Klarna stats: 2.3M conversations, 700 agents, $60M saved. "They had to course-correct on quality — which is why the first shift matters."
- Sequoia thesis: "The addressable market isn't $600B software. It's $10T services."
- "Price for outcomes → compete against labor costs, not software budgets. That's why they scale so fast."

### Slide 7: Agentic Security
- "The fourth shift — the elephant in the room."
- Acknowledge the gap honestly: 21% governance maturity, 40% failure rate prediction.
- "But it's getting better, fast." Walk through the patterns.
- Land the punchline: "Agents with audit trails are MORE auditable than humans. When an agent decides, you can replay exactly why. When a human decides, you get a Post-it note."

### Slide 8: What This Means for You
- "Four mental shifts. What do you actually do with this?"
- Three takeaways — give each one 30-40 seconds:
  1. Re-ask: "Don't add AI to your category. Re-ask the category's question."
  2. Write path: "Features suggest. Products execute. 'I did it' vs 'here's what I found.'"
  3. Intelligence moat: "The model is a commodity. Your moat is what you learn from every interaction."
- Don't rush. This is where the audience goes from "interesting" to "I know what to do."

### Slide 9: StackOne CTA
- Shift to warm, direct tone.
- "If you're building this kind of company — come talk to me."
- "We built StackOne to be the integration infrastructure for this next generation. I'd love to hear what you're building."
- 20-30 seconds max. Invitation, not a pitch. Then move to close.

### Slide 10: Close
- Deliver slowly.
- "The question isn't whether your category will be re-asked. It is."
- "The question is whether you're the one asking it."
- Silence. Let the Q&A begin. Leave this slide up.
